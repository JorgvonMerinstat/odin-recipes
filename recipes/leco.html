Lecsó

Description

Lecsó is a popular dish in the Czech Republic, though it is often met with mixed feelings due to its association with school cafeterias. Despite this, lecsó is a tasty and versatile meal that combines peppers, tomatoes, and onions with a touch of paprika. This dish is enjoyed across Central Europe, where it is known for its simple, comforting flavors. Historically, lecsó has its roots in Hungarian cuisine, where it is known as "lecsó." It was traditionally a way to use up summer vegetables and is often served with bread or as a side dish to meats.

Ingredients (for 4-6 persons)

  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 bell peppers, sliced (preferably a mix of colors)
  • 4 large tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika (smoked paprika is recommended)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2 sausages, sliced (for added protein)

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the sliced bell peppers and cook until they start to soften.
  5. Add the diced tomatoes and paprika. Stir well to combine.
  6. If using sausages, add them now and stir to incorporate.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the flavors are well blended.
  9. Serve hot, with bread or as a side dish to meats.
