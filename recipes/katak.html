Butcher's Dish

Description

Butcher's Dish, known as "Katův šleh" in Czech, is a traditional Czech meal. The name "kat" translates to "executioner" in English, and "šleh" can mean "strike" or "whip," giving a colorful description of this hearty dish. The origin of the name is somewhat ambiguous, but it is believed that this dish was named humorously after the traditional butchery skills required to prepare it. This dish typically features a mixture of meat, onions, and spices, creating a flavorful and satisfying meal that has become a staple in Czech cuisine.

Ingredients (for 4-6 persons)

  • 500 g of beef or pork, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika (smoked paprika is recommended)
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon of flour (for thickening)
  • For serving: 500 g of frozen or fresh French fries

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the meat cubes and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  5. Sprinkle the paprika over the meat and stir to combine.
  6. Pour in the beef broth and bring to a simmer.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. If desired, add a tablespoon of flour to thicken the sauce.
  9. Simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  10. While the meat is simmering, prepare the French fries according to the package instructions or fry fresh potatoes.
  11. Serve the Butcher's Dish hot with crispy French fries.
